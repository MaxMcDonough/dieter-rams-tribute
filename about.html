Biography

The man, in brief.

Dieter Rams is one of the most influential product designers of the twentieth century. His work at Braun — spanning over four decades — produced some of the most enduring objects in design history.

Born in Wiesbaden in 1932, Rams trained as an architect and interior designer before joining Braun in 1955, where he would eventually rise to become head of design. Alongside his work at Braun he simultaneously ran the design department of Vitsœ, the furniture company, from 1959 onwards.

His belief that good design should be invisible — serving the user without demanding attention — shaped not only his own practice, but the broader trajectory of industrial design. His work influenced a generation of designers, most notably Jonathan Ive and the design language of Apple.

Since retiring from Braun in 1997, Rams has continued to advocate for sustainable, considered design through lectures, exhibitions, and his ongoing relationship with Vitsœ.

Event Born in Wiesbaden

20 May 1932. His grandfather, a carpenter, teaches him the value of craft and the discipline of making things well.

01 / 11
  1. 20 May 1932. His grandfather, a carpenter, teaches him the value of craft and the discipline of making things well.
  2. Studies architecture and interior design, interrupted by a practical apprenticeship as a carpenter.
  3. Hired as an architect to redesign Braun's Frankfurt offices. His talent is recognised and he moves into product design.
  4. Co-designs the SK 4 with Hans Gugelot. The clear acrylic lid and flat white case declare the intent of the entire Braun programme.
  5. Designs the 606 Universal Shelving System for Vitsœ, beginning a parallel sixty-year relationship with the furniture company.
  6. Appointed Director of Design — a role he holds for three decades and uses to build one of the most coherent corporate design programmes in history.
  7. Articulates his ten principles of good design, providing the field with its most enduring ethical framework.
  8. Releases the ET 66 calculator and AB 312 alarm clock with Dietrich Lubs — late-career proofs that restraint still has more to say.
  9. Leaves Braun after 42 years. His design language at the company had by this point been largely abandoned for market-driven aesthetics.
  10. Gary Hustwit releases Rams, a film documenting his life and philosophy. Introduces his work to a new generation.
  11. Continues to lecture and write on design, sustainability, and the role of the designer in a world overwhelmed by objects. The 606 and 620 remain in production, unchanged.

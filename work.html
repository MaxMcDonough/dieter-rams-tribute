Selected Work · Nine Objects in Detail

The work, examined.

Nine of Rams's most consequential designs, dismantled to their structural decisions. Read in order — they trace the construction of an entire visual language across three decades, from the Snow White's Coffin to the calculator that taught Cupertino how to lay out a keypad.

01Hi-Fi Audio · Braun

SK 4 Phonosuper

1956 · with Hans Gugelot

The "Snow White's Coffin" did the unthinkable in 1956: it hid nothing. A clear acrylic lid replaced the wood-and-fabric box of every prior radiogram. Form became inventory — the user could see what they owned. The visual industry hasn't recovered since.

Material
Steel · Acrylic · Birch
Production
1956 – 1962
Innovation
First clear-lid hi-fi
Held by
MoMA · V&A
Cat. 01 · 1956
02Portable Radio · Braun

T 3 Pocket Radio

1958

The first pocket transistor radio to refuse a knob. A perforated speaker grille became the entire face — the form Jonathan Ive cited as the direct ancestor of the original iPod, forty-three years later. Less to look at; more to use.

Material
Plastic · Steel grille
Production
1958 – 1963
Interface
Sliding switch · Dial
Influence
1st-gen iPod (2001)
Cat. 02 · 1958
03Hi-Fi Speaker · Braun

L 2 Speaker

1958

The first consumer speaker to treat the grille as the entire design. Uniform perforated metal across the front face — no badge, no name, no decoration. The visual vocabulary recurred across the Braun catalogue for the next thirty years.

Material
Perforated steel · Wood
Production
1958 – 1962
Form
Front-grille
Held by
Vitra Design Museum
Cat. 03 · 1958
04Furniture System · Vitsœ

606 Universal Shelving System

1960 · still in production

A wall-mounted E-track and a vocabulary of shelves, cabinets, and tables that can be rebuilt to any wall, any home, any life stage. Sold once, kept forever, added to incrementally. The most radical idea in furniture remains the rejection of the set.

Manufacturer
Vitsœ, since 1960
Configurations
Effectively infinite
Disassembly
Designed in
Status
Continuous production
Cat. 04 · 1960
05Modular Seating · Vitsœ

620 Chair Programme

1962

Furniture as system. Seat units that connect into sofas, lounges, or single chairs and reconfigure with the household. The same pieces buy you a single armchair at twenty-two and an L-shaped sofa at forty-five — no waste, no replacement cycle.

Manufacturer
Vitsœ
Form
Modular seat units
Lifecycle
Reconfigurable

Cat. 05 · 1962
06World Band Radio · Braun

T 1000 World Receiver

1963

A professional shortwave receiver with the discipline of a measurement instrument. Every frequency dial, every signal-strength meter, every band selector arranged on a single grid. The case taught a generation of engineers what an interface should look like.

Range
AM · FM · 8 SW bands
Production
1963 – 1981
Form
Symmetrical grid
Held by
MoMA · Vitra
Cat. 06 · 1963
07Kitchen Appliance · Braun

Citromatic MPZ 21

1972 · with Jürgen Greubel

Rams's design system entering the breakfast table. A domed white housing, a clear lid, a press-down juicing action — appliance design treated with the same restraint as the hi-fi catalogue. Still cited as the benchmark for kitchenware proportion.

Material
ABS plastic · Glass lid
Co-designer
Jürgen Greubel
Form
Domed
Status
Reissued 2010s
Cat. 07 · 1972
08Timepiece · Braun

AB 312 Alarm Clock

1987 · with Dietrich Lubs

A clock reduced to reading and setting the time. The numerals — a custom typeface drawn for the dial — sit on a clean white face with no visual noise around them. The clearest argument that utility and beauty are not in opposition; they are the same thing.

Co-designer
Dietrich Lubs
Movement
Quartz analog
Numeral type
Custom-drawn
Held by
MoMA
Cat. 08 · 1987
09Calculator · Braun

ET 66 Pocket Calculator

1987 · with Dietrich Lubs

Three colour decisions did the work of an instruction manual. Numbers in grey, operations in black, equals in Braun-orange. Apple's iOS Calculator borrowed the layout almost cell-for-cell — three decades after the original shipped.

Co-designer
Dietrich Lubs
Influence on
iOS Calculator (2008)
Display
8-digit LCD
Held by
MoMA · Vitra
Cat. 09 · 1987

braun Part II · Selection
II · Why Dieter Rams

No shortlist.

The minute the brief asked for a designer who shaped how I think about the work, the answer was Rams.

The entry point

I came to Rams through Apple. Before I knew his name, I knew the iPod's click wheel, the iMac G4's swivel arm, the iPhone's calculator. When I learned Jonathan Ive had cited Rams as his single greatest influence for two decades, the connection clicked.

The 1958 T 3 radio → the 2001 iPod. The 1987 ET 66 calculator → iOS Calculator in 2008. The same keypad logic, forty-three years apart.

Why his framework, specifically

Rams's ten principles, written in 1976, remain the closest the field of industrial design has ever come to an ethical compass. Not a style. Not a movement. A test.

"Is my design good design?" — and a way to answer.

The Lineage

Same keypad. Forty-three years apart.

Braun · 1958 T 3
Pocket Radio The first pocket transistor radio to refuse a knob. A perforated speaker grille became the entire face. Sliding power switch, recessed dial — nothing else.
→
43 years
Apple · 2001 iPod
1st Generation White plastic face, centred circular control, recessed screen. Jonathan Ive has repeatedly cited the T 3 as the direct ancestor.

The lineage repeats across the catalogue: ET 66 → iOS Calculator (2008) · L 2 Speaker → HomePod (2018) · 606 Shelving → Apple Park interiors.

braun Part II · What I've Internalised
Personal Influence on Practice

Three things from Rams
I now carry in my work.

01 · Testable restraint

"Less, but better" isn't a feeling. You can audit a design against the ten principles and get a verdict. Changes how I critique my own work. Every project this year has gotten shorter, quieter, more specific because of him.

02 · Form is inventory

The SK 4's clear lid in 1956 was radical because it refused to hide what was inside. Good design shows the user what they own. I think about that every time I'm tempted to disguise a UI.

03 · Long-lasting is praise

The 606 shelf has been in continuous production since 1960. The 620 chair since 1962. The opposite of the throwaway design economy. The brief I want my own work to meet.

So this project isn't only documentation. It's the longest I've spent inside one designer's head, deliberately — and the site is the artefact of that residency.

braun Part III · The Thesis
The Thesis

Good design is testable.

If the work is the proof of the principles, the medium that documents the work should be the same proof — scaled to the screen.

The site I built doesn't only describe Rams's principles. It tries to embody them. Every interactive section is built around an actual Braun product as its interface metaphor — the calculator, the tuning dial, the studio meter, the hi-fi rack. The metaphor isn't decoration. It's the navigation.

braun Part IV · What the Research Told Me
Research → Build

Three takeaways
that survived to the build.

01 / 03 Principles first, biography second. The journey map showed nearly every visitor's primary intent is "remind me what the ten principles are." Wireframe priority: principles directly after about, before the work catalogue.
02 / 03 Loud references, quiet layout. Precedent research showed Vitsœ's own site is genuinely restrained — small type, generous white space, a single accent. The design board codified this: one accent, one type family, one grid.
03 / 03 Objects, never ornament. Mood board explicitly excluded lifestyle photography that romanticises the work. Every product image on the final site is on a clean off-white "catalogue plate" with a thin inner border — Vitsœ's own treatment.

Every structural decision in the build traces back to one of these. Research wasn't a box to tick. It was the frame that made the design possible.

V · Typography

One family.
Three voices.

Sans — IBM Plex Sans

Aa

All UI — navigation, body copy, captions, meta strips. Weights 200 · 400 · 500 · 600.

Serif — IBM Plex Serif Italic

Aa

Editorial moments — chapter titles, principle headlines, pull quotes, the closing mantra.

Mono — IBM Plex Mono

Aa

Readouts, LED labels, kbd keys, colophon, colour swatches — every moment pretending to be an instrument.

IBM commissioned Plex to replace Helvetica with a typeface that has personality but stays out of the way. Exactly the Rams brief — a typographic translation of "less, but better." Free under SIL OFL, served via Google Fonts.

braun Part V · The Device Language
V · The Device Language

Six products,
one catalogue of materials.

The most consequential design decision in the project: every interactive section is wrapped in chrome that reads as a real Braun device. Same dark gradient frame, same lowercase wordmark, same orange LED, same monospace meta strip. This is what knits the whole site together.

Shared CSS tokens
Device body--device-bg · dark linear gradient
Inner window--device-window · #0e0d0a
Frame edge--device-border · rgba(255,255,255,0.08)
Separator--device-line · rgba(255,255,255,0.06)
Texture--device-grille · repeating-linear-gradient
LED on--device-led-on · var(--accent)
Where the tokens are used
  • Hero — Braun device faceplate wrapping the name
  • Principles — ET 66 calculator frame + display panel
  • Timeline — T 1000 World Receiver tuning dial casing
  • Index — Studio mixer meter-rack frame
  • Legacy — Six-channel hi-fi rack
  • HUD — Tape-deck readout pinned bottom of every page

Define the materials once. Reuse them on every device. This is exactly how Braun's actual industrial design programme worked under Rams.

braun Part VI · Phase 2 · 21st.dev Plugins
Phase 02 · v2

Four React components,
adapted to vanilla.

21st.dev is an open-source community-curated marketplace of React/Tailwind UI components. Each ships as a single TSX file. My host is vanilla, so adoption meant rewriting each one — keeping the visual idea, losing the React abstraction.

PLUGIN 01 Dot-Pattern Quote Block Bordered frame with corner squares and a dot-grid background. Mixed-weight typographic statement. Still lives in work.html between chapter intro and the objects. Adapted · still in site
PLUGIN 02 Parallax Scroll Feature Alternating left/right rows with scroll-driven copy slide-up and image clip-reveal. Powers the entire Work chapter — nine objects in detail. Adapted · core component
PLUGIN 03 Wave-Path Interactive Line Spring-physics SVG line that bends with cursor and snaps back. Originally lived before contact but removed in v4 — metaphor was too abstract. Replaced by the Eleventh Principle. Removed · superseded
PLUGIN 04 Text-Rotate Scroll Gallery Sticky left pane with character-rotating text, synced to scrolling cards on the right. Powers Designers He Shaped — 7 contemporary designers, each with a hand-drawn SVG silhouette of their signature work. Adapted · core component

What the adaptation taught me: word-level wrapping, character-level animation. The first text-rotate broke "Jonathan Ive" mid-word. Fix: wrap each word in an unbreakable inline-block, animate chars inside.

braun Part VII · Walkthrough · Cover
Page 01 · index.html

Cover.
Two things only.

The cover introduces the subject (the Braun-framed name) and gives the reader the table of contents. Nothing else. No long-form copy. No "about Max." A single hero, a five-row contents block, one line of credit.

The page exists to orient, not to perform. The most explicit application of "less, but better" anywhere in the site.

What's on the page
  • Full-viewport hero · modular-grid background drifting subtly
  • Braun device faceplate wrapping the name
  • "Dieter Rams" in sans-light 112pt · "Less, but better" in serif italic
  • Era · Origin · Studio readout strip along the device bottom
  • Single chapter-contents list · 5 rows linking to Chapter I–V
  • Footer with credit + keyboard hint
  • Persistent chapter rail and HUD pinned at the edges

Ten principles.
Ten keys.

The hardest page to design. The principles are the most-quoted text in industrial design — every Rams tribute lays them out as a numbered list. I needed a treatment that wasn't a list.

braun Part IX · Reflection
IX · What I Learned

Three things
I didn't know going in.

01 · LOAD-BEARING METAPHORS

Skeuomorphism gets a bad name because it's usually decorative — leather calendars, felt poker tables. What I learned: skeuomorphism works when the metaphor does real navigational work. The calculator keypad is an actual interface for choosing a principle. The dial is an actual interface for moving through a timeline. Not decoration — structure.

02 · HONOUR BY APPLYING

My first instinct was to add. Another section, another readout, another animation. The most consequential edits in this project were all subtractions — deleting the original Work grid, replacing the wave Discipline Test with a single-input form, splitting the long scroll into focused pages. Every removal made the site closer to what Rams actually argued for.

03 · AI CHANGES THE TEMPO

Working with Claude Code let me try and discard ideas at a speed I couldn't on my own. The first meter rack used identical dials — wrong. We replaced it with six purpose-built instruments inside the same hour. Bad ideas that would normally take a week to admit and remove took a half-day. A different working method — worth being honest about.

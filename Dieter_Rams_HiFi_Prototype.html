01 — About
Architect of Quiet Objects
DR

Dieter Rams is one of the most influential industrial designers of the twentieth century. Trained as an architect and carpenter, he joined Braun in 1955 and rose to become Head of Design — a role he held for over three decades, producing some of the most enduring objects in design history.

His grandfather, a master carpenter, instilled a belief in craftsmanship and honest materials. This early influence shaped a philosophy centered on restraint and purpose — the conviction that good design should serve the user without demanding attention.

02 — Principles
Ten Principles of Good Design
01

Good design is innovative

The possibilities for innovation are not, by any means, exhausted. Technological development is always offering new opportunities for innovative design.

02

Good design makes a product useful

A product is bought to be used. It has to satisfy certain criteria, not only functional, but also psychological and aesthetic.

03

Good design is aesthetic

The aesthetic quality of a product is integral to its usefulness because products we use every day affect our person and our well-being.

04

Good design makes a product understandable

It clarifies the product’s structure. Better still, it can make the product talk. At best, it is self-explanatory.

05

Good design is unobtrusive

Products fulfilling a purpose are like tools. They are neither decorative objects nor works of art. Their design should therefore be both neutral and restrained.

06

Good design is honest

It does not make a product more innovative, powerful or valuable than it really is. It does not attempt to manipulate the consumer with promises that cannot be kept.

07

Good design is long-lasting

It avoids being fashionable and therefore never appears antiquated. Unlike fashionable design, it lasts many years.

08

Good design is thorough down to the last detail

Nothing must be arbitrary or left to chance. Care and accuracy in the design process show respect towards the user.

09

Good design is environmentally friendly

Design makes an important contribution to the preservation of the environment. It conserves resources and minimises physical and visual pollution.

10

Good design is as little design as possible

Less, but better — because it concentrates on the essential aspects, and the products are not burdened with non-essentials. Back to purity, back to simplicity.

04 — Legacy
Enduring Influence
◼

Apple & Digital Design

Jonathan Ive has cited Rams as his primary influence. The iPhone, iMac, and Apple’s entire design language trace directly to Rams’s principles.

▲

Architecture

His belief in honest materials, structural clarity, and elimination of ornament influenced a generation of minimalist architects.

◆

Sustainability

Rams was advocating for durable, repairable products decades before sustainability became a design movement. The 606 remains in production unchanged.

◎

Design Education

The Ten Principles are taught in nearly every design program worldwide, forming the ethical foundation for generations of designers.

◯

User Experience

“Indifference towards people is the cardinal sin in design.” This user-centered ethos predates the UX field by decades.

■

Furniture & Interiors

The 606 Universal Shelving System and 620 Chair established a modular, system-based approach that defined modern furniture design.

05 — Timeline
A Life in Design
1932

Born in Wiesbaden, Germany

Born into a family of craftsmen. His grandfather, a master carpenter, becomes his earliest and most lasting influence.

1947

Carpentry Apprenticeship

Begins learning the craft of woodworking, developing a deep understanding of materials, joinery, and honest construction.

1953

Architecture Studies

Graduates from the Wiesbaden School of Art with a degree in architecture and interior decoration.

1955

Joins Braun

Hired as an architect to redesign the Braun offices. Quickly pivots to product design under the mentorship of the Braun brothers.

1956

SK 4 “Snow White’s Coffin”

Co-designs the revolutionary SK 4 radiogram with Hans Gugelot. Its clear acrylic lid becomes an icon of modern design.

1961

Head of Design at Braun

Appointed chief design officer, beginning the golden era of Braun products and establishing a unified design language.

1960

606 Universal Shelving System

Designs the 606 for Vitsoe. Still manufactured unchanged today — the longest-produced piece of modern furniture.

1976

Ten Principles of Good Design

Codifies his design philosophy into ten principles that become the most enduring ethical framework in the field.

1997

Retirement from Braun

Retires after 42 years, having designed over 500 products and shaped the visual language of an entire industry.

2018

“Rams” Documentary

Gary Hustwit’s documentary introduces Rams’s philosophy to a new generation, cementing his legacy as design’s moral compass.
